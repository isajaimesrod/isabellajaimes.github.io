About

Researching the worlds built around care, migration, and platforms.

I am a PhD student in Information at the University of Toronto and a researcher at STREET Lab. My work combines feminist STS, CSCW, ethnography, and collaborative design to study platform labour, domestic work, mobility, and migrant infrastructures.

My research explores how Latina migrant workers navigate digital labour markets, informal economies, and technological systems that shape visibility, value, and survival.

Across projects on domestic work platforms, ride-hailing, and digital cultures, I examine how technologies reorganize labour and everyday life while communities simultaneously build alternative infrastructures of care and resistance.

I work across qualitative and computational methods, combining ethnography, workshops, interviews, platform analysis, mapping, and participatory research practices.

Featured Research

Research stories.

El costo de la independencia

Ethnographic research with Latina house-cleaners in Toronto examining how workers navigate asynchronous marketplaces, agencies, informal economies, and digital infrastructures while building autonomy and networks of care.

The project explores how platforms shape labour conditions, visibility, and mobility, while workers develop situated strategies of resistance and survival.

Networks of Care

Collaborative research investigating how migrant domestic workers build infrastructures of support through WhatsApp groups, shared knowledges, spreadsheets, and informal networks that exceed platform systems.

This work examines care not only as labour, but as an infrastructural and collective practice.
