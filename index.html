About

Feminist STS research on platforms, labour, and migrant infrastructures.

I am a PhD student in Information at the University of Toronto and a researcher affiliated with STREET Lab. My work examines how digital platforms, algorithmic systems, and technological infrastructures shape labour, mobility, care, and everyday life across migrant and Latin American contexts.

My research sits at the intersection of Science and Technology Studies (STS), CSCW, feminist technoscience, and digital labour studies. I combine ethnography, participatory design, computational methods, and collaborative research practices to investigate how workers navigate platform economies and precarious technological systems.

Across projects on domestic work, ride-hailing, social media cultures, and digital migration infrastructures, I explore how communities build alternative forms of care, solidarity, and resistance within conditions of inequality and platform-mediated labour.

My work has been presented at ACM CSCW, CHI, COMPASS, STS Italia, AoIR, and the Society for Social Studies of Science (4S), and published in venues including Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction, CHI, and Critical Sociology.

Before beginning my PhD, I completed an MA in Science and Technology Studies at York University and dual undergraduate degrees in Sociology and Political Science at Universidad del Rosario in Bogotá, Colombia.

Featured Research

Research stories.

El costo de la independencia

Ethnographic research with Latina migrant house-cleaners in Toronto examining how workers navigate digital labour markets, asynchronous marketplaces, agencies, and informal economies while building autonomy and infrastructures of care.

Through interviews and collaborative research practices, this project explores how platforms mediate visibility, mobility, and labour conditions while workers develop situated strategies of survival and resistance.

Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction · CSCW 2024

Read publication

Networks of Care

Collaborative research investigating how migrant domestic workers build systems of support through WhatsApp groups, shared spreadsheets, informal infrastructures, and collective forms of knowledge beyond platform systems.

This work examines care not only as labour, but as a technological and collective practice grounded in feminist and migrant solidarities.

CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems · 2024

Read publication

Mapping Movement

Research on ride-hailing drivers in Bogotá exploring how urban mobility, algorithmic management, and platform infrastructures shape everyday experiences of labour and movement in the city.

Drawing on feminist STS approaches, this project investigates mobility as both technological governance and future-making practice within platform economies.

STS Italia 2025 · 4S 2025
