About me

research · storytelling · design

I am an STS and CSCW researcher whose work focuses on how technology shapes and mediates relations within immigrant Latinx communities. My research sits at the intersection of technology, labour, and social justice, with a particular interest in domestic and care work, informal economies, and platformed forms of expertise.

I am part of the Socio-technical Resistance and Ethical Technologies (STREET) Lab at the University of Toronto, where I collaborate on participatory and design-based projects with migrant workers and community organizations.

Research

Canada · Colombia & Latin America

Canada

Ongoing work with Latina house-cleaners in Toronto and platform-based domestic labour economies.

El costo de la independencia

I investigate how the feelings of Latina house-cleaners in Toronto are mediated by digital marketplaces. Through 19 interviews, I explore how workers use platforms for flexibility while navigating precarity, and how collective identity fosters solidarity and sororidades.

current project

Networks of care in digital domestic labour economies

With collaborators at STREET Lab, I examine how domestic workers build networks of care, support and resistance within platform infrastructures, and how these networks reconfigure ideas of expertise and professionalism.

collaborative · CHI 2024

Colombia & Latin America

Earlier work on ride-hailing, domestic work platforms, and digital cultures of health in Colombia.

Domestic Work Platforms in Colombia

We investigated how domestic work platforms in Colombia affect workers' employment and social relations through algorithmic governance. Using ethnographic methods and interviews, we analysed matching, ranking and pricing systems to inform more equitable platform designs for Latin America.

completed · Colombia

Mapping Movement

This project explores how digital platforms shape ride-hailing driver experiences in Bogotá, revealing the interplay of technology, socio-spatial dynamics, and drivers' adaptive strategies that challenge algorithmic control and reshape urban mobility practices.

completed · Bogotá

Between Compliance and Resistance

A study of how social media platforms mediate conversations around fitness, health and mental health among young people in Colombia. We trace how different platforms encourage distinct forms of credibility, storytelling, and gendered digital violence.

completed · social media
